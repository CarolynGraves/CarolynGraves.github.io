Q.1. What Is a VPN and What Does It Do?

A. A virtual private network (VPN) is a type of network that allows you to create a secure connection over the internet to protect your private data and access restricted websites. A VPN works by routing your web traffic through a secure tunnel to a remote server, making it appear as if your connection is coming from that server, rather than your computer. This allows you to access content that may be blocked in your location, as well as protect your data from hackers and malicious software.

Q.2. Why Should I Use a VPN for my PC with Windows 7?

Additionally, a VPN can help protect you from malicious software, such as viruses and spyware, and can even help you avoid being scammed online.
