1. Definition of Virtual Private Network in computing#

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) is a secure network connection across a public network, such as the Internet. It allows a user to access a private network and share data remotely through public networks, while maintaining the security of a private network. VPNs encrypt data to provide greater security than regular networks and enable users to stay anonymous while browsing online. With a VPN, users can access web content, applications, and files securely on remote networks, while their data remains private. VPNs also provide a way for users to access networks in restricted areas, such as firewalls or other protected networks. VPNs are most commonly used to access corporate networks, but they are also used by individuals to protect their data and stay anonymous while browsing the web.
